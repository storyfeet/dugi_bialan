Dugi Bialan - Grammar

Grammar

Overview

Dugi Bialan is a minimalist language seeking balance between simplicity and minimalism with ease of communication, for both speaker and listener.

It currently has close to 200 words, and the majority of meanings can be created by combining these. There will be more words added slowly, and carefully in future. Enough to enable species identification and some difficult concepts, but never more than 240. (Few enough to address the whole word list and alphabet within a byte)

The language has 5 types of words.

  • Particles (PAR)
  • Conjunctions (CON)
  • Auxiliary (AUX)
  • Prefixes (PRE)
  • Atoms (ATOM)

To aid the listener/reader, each type of word is only used as one of these things, and cannot be confused with the others.

The particle types are marked with each word in the word list. Words without a marking are ATOMs

Particles are used to mark the role of whatever follows them takes in the sentence. It no particle is given at the beginning of the sentence, the particle " оҲЈ" (' ye ') is assumed, meaning that the first words will be the "subject" of the sentence.

Meaning is built by combining Atoms (and Prefixed Atoms) to describe an idea. Depending on the Particle before it, the idea is considered to be either:

  • The Particles " оҲЈ / о…і / оҮғ / оҶў / о…Һ / о…Ҹ" (' ye / ei / lon / gin / da / dan ') are followed by.
    • A noun phrase
    • A sub-clause
  • The Particle " оҶҫ" (' li ') is followed by:
    • An adjective phrase
      eg : "о…•о…ү" (' dei buna ') "much good/ very good"
    • A quantifier and a noun phrase.
      eg : "оҶЎоҲЁ" (' gi zan ') "a person".
  • The Particle " оҲ’" (' wei ') is followed by
    • A verb / verb phrase.
      eg : "оҲҹоҮ•" (' yawin meni ') "going back"

The particle-idea pairs can be put together in any order to make a sentence. Verbs are not necessarily needed. For example :

"о„ҙ о…ҺоҲҠ о…іо…№оҲҖо…Ў" (' bei da vin ei elo vedin dis ') or "I to you - pen this"

Is equivalent to :

" о…іо…№оҲҖо…Ў о…ҺоҲҠ оҲЈо„ҙ" (' ei elo vedin dis da vin ye bei ') " - pen this TO you SUBJECT I"

Note that in the second sentence, because the subject was not at the beginning, it was prefaced with " оҲЈ" (' ye '). Even though neither sentence has a verb, the meaning is clear.

If a verb is needed, " оҲ’" (' wei ') is used to mark the simple form. It can be roughly translated as "do".

"о„ҙ оҲ’о„ј о…іо…№ о…ҺоҲҠ" (' bei wei bena ei elo da vin ') or "I DO give - tool TO you"

If instead an adjective is needed " оҶҫ" (' li ') is used, to mark the "being" form. It can be roughly translated as "am/is".

"оҲҠ оҶҫо…ү" (' vin li buna ') or "you ARE good".

Conjunctions (CON) join sentences together into with "if" and "context".

"оҲҠ оҶҫо…үоҶҠоғёо„ҙ о…ҺоҲҠ о…іо…№оҲҖо…Ў" (' vin li buna esi, bei da vin ei elo vedin dis ') "you ARE good IF I TO you - this pen"

Note the "оҶҠ" (' esi ') "A esi B" means "if A then B". So the meaning is "If you are good, I'll give you this pen.

Auxiliaries (AUX) are a small set of special words that can be used in a variety of ways that other words cannot.

Prefixes (PRE) must always have an item after them to complete their meaning. If that item is "оҲІ" (' zo ') it means the concept that item represents . For example "оҶҸоҮұ" (' ga-una ') means "colour of mouth" or "red" but "оҶҸоҲІ" (' ga-zo ') means "colour"

Sentences can be negated by prefixing the relevant Particle with 'va'

  • "о„ҙоҮҪ оҶҫо…ү" (' bei va-li buna ') "I am not good"
  • "о„ҙоҮҪ оҲ’оҲі" (' bei va-wei zon ') "I don't know"
  • "о„ҙоҮҪ оҶўо…ЎоҮқ" (' bei va-gin dis mon ') I'm not with him"

Yes-no questions are asked by using the "particle not-particle" structure.

  • "оҲҠ оҶҫоҮҪ оҶҫо…ү" (' vin li va-li buna ') : "you are not-are good?" / "are you good?"
  • "оҲҠ оҲ’оҮҪ оҲ’оҲҹ" (' vin wei va-wei yawin ') : "you do not-do go?" / "are you going?"
  • "оҲҠ оҲ’оҶ• оҶўоҮҪ оҶўо„ҙ" (' vin wei gein gin va-gin bei ') : "you go with not-with me?" / "can I come with you?"

Any particle can be turned into a yes-no question like this, though only one per sentence.

Information questions can be asked by replacing the missing information with the word "оҲҮ" (' vemi ') or "what".

  • "оҲҠ о…Һо„ҙ оҲ’о„ј о…іоҲҮ" (' vin da bei wei bena ei vemi ') "you to me give what?"
  • "оҲЁоҲҮ оҲ’о…Ҙ" (' zan vemi wei dugi ') "person what do speak" / "who's speaking"

Formal Sentence Structure

A simple sentence in Dugi Bialan is a list of Particles, each followed by a set of atoms.

Word : PRE* (ATOM | PRE\2)

A Word is either an Atom, or set of Prefixes followed by an Atom.

  • "о…Ұ" (' dumo ') : Room
  • "оҶ©оҲІ" (' guli-zo ') : body
  • "оҶҸоҮҪоҲІ" (' ga-va-zo ') : Colour of nothing / black

Item : Word+ ("bi" Complex +)*

An Item is a list of words. Latter words act as an adjective/adverb everything before them. "о……" (' bi '), acts to create a separation, so that everything after it is treated becomes a single word.

  • "о…¶оҶҸоҮұ" (' elin ga-una ') : clothes red
  • "о…№о…әоҮЈо…•" (' elo ema-musi dei ') : tool music big - big instrument
  • "о…№о……о…әоҮЈо…•" (' elo bi ema-musi dei ') : tool of (music big) - loud instrument
  • "о…Ұо……о…ҳоҶ©оҲІ" (' dumo bi delo guli-zo ') : Room of wash body / bathroom
  • "оҲҢоҲ–о…өо……оҲ’оҮһ о…іоҶ–оҲІо„ҙ" (' vo-wuwi elesa bi wei mugo ei gela-zo bei ') "the cat that ate my fish"

Complex : Item | Strict

A Complex-Item can be either an Item as above, or a whole strict sentence, where every part is marked correctly.

Strict sentences always begin with a particle.

  • " оҲЈоҲҠ оҲ’оҲҹ" (' ye vin wei yawin '). or "you go"
  • " оҲ’оҮһ" (' wei mugo ') "do eat"
  • " о…Һо„ҙ о…іо…Ў" (' da bei ei dis ') "to me this"

Each of these could follow "о„ҙ оҲ’о„» о…і" (' bei wei belin ei ') "I want".

  • "о„ҙ оҲ’о„» о…іоҲЈоҲҠ оҲ’оҲҹ" (' bei wei belin ei ye vin wei yawin ') "I want THAT you go" / "I want you to go"
  • "о„ҙ оҲ’о„» о…іоҲ’оҮһ" (' bei wei belin ei wei mugo ') "I want THAT do eat" / "I want to eat"
  • "о„ҙ оҲ’о„» о…іо…Һо„ҙ о…іо…Ў" (' bei wei belin ei da bei ei dis ') "I want THAT to me - this" / "I want this [to come] to me"

Question : P:(Particle) "va" $P

A Question is at least one particle then "оҮҪ" (' va ') "not" then the same particles again.

  • " оҲ’оҮҪ оҲ’" (' wei va-wei ') "do you? / does it?"
  • " оҶҫоҮҪ оҶҫ" (' li va-li ') "are you? / is it?"
  • " оҶўоҮҪ оҶў" (' gin va-gin ') "are you with? / is it with?"

Phrase : (Marker Complex) | (QuestionMark Complex)

A Phrase is a marker, followed by the Item it marks together, this can either be a simple concept, or a whole sentence.

  • " оҲ’оҲҹ" (' wei yawin ') : VERB - go
  • " оҶҫо„»о…Ҝ" (' li belin egi ') : IS feeling-bad
  • " о…ҺоҲЁо…ӣ" (' da zan deni ') : TO person-little : To the child
  • " оҮғоҲқо……оҮ”о„ҙ" (' lon yasin bi mengi bei ') ON way of house my : On the way home

Sentence : Item? Phrase+ | ["bei"] Item? Phrase*

A sentence may begin with an unmarked Item, that will be the subject of the sentence.

If a sentence begins with "о„ҙ" (' bei '), then it can be followed with an item representing a verb

Strict : Phrase+

A strict sentence has no implied markers, a subject is marked with " оҲЈ" (' ye '). "о„ҙ" (' bei ') may not be followed directly by a verb.

Joined : ((Item | Sentence) CON)* Sentence

Sentences may be joined with a Conjunction, making the latter sentence dependent on the prior. Items can also be used for establishing contenxt.

  • "о…үоҶҠоҮӨо„ј о…Һо„ҙ о…іоҮһ" (' buna esi o bena da bei ei mugo ') "If good, oh give me good." / "Please give me food"
  • "оҶ•оҶ®о„ҙ оҲ’оҶ•оҮһ" (' gein la bei wei gein mugo ') "Maybe I can eat"

Primary Particles

Particles are how dugi bialan marks the beginning and end of compound items. They also declare the role of the following item in the sentence.

These are :

  • "ye" : subject : followed by a noun.
    dropped if sentence starts with subject
  • "wei" : do : followed by a verb
  • "li" : is : followed by an adjective, or noun.
  • "lon" : in/at : followed by a location, or a state of being
  • "ei" : direct object : followed by a noun
  • "da" : towards/to/for : followed by a noun
  • "dan" : because/from : followed by a noun or adjective
  • "go" : saying/quote : followed by the quoted clause (use "")
  • "o" : Imperetive/Vocative/Polite Call : followed by either a verb or someone to call politely

It is important to note that the same word can be a noun, verb or adjective.

-- "о„ҙ оҲ’оҮһ" (' bei wei mugo ')
.. I eat
?? I DO food

-- "о„ҙ оҶҫоҮһ" (' bei li mugo ')
.. I am food

-- "о„ҙ оҲ’оҲғ о…іоҲғ" (' bei wei vei ei vei ')
.. I set alight a fire

-- "о„ҙ оҶҫоҲғ" (' bei li vei ')
.. I am hot

-- "о„ҙ оҶҫоҶЎоҲғ" (' bei li gi vei ')
.. I am a fire

Particle pairs create Sub-Clauses

Because a Complex item may be sub-clause, and sub-clauses must always be Strict Sentences that begin with a "Particle", Two particles together will always create a sub clause.

da li : to be / in order to be

-- "о„ҙ оҲ’оҮһ о…іоҲҗоҮһ о…ҺоҶҫоҲҘ" (' bei wei mugo ei wa-mugo da li yowa ')
.. I eat vegetables to become strong
?? I DO eat OBJECT vegetable TO BE strong

da wei : to do / in order to

-- "оҲЁо…Ў оҲ’о…Ҳ о…ҺоҲ’о„јоҮһ о…іоҲ°о…Ў" (' zan dis wei buli da wei bena mugo ei zi dis ')
.. this person works to feed his children ?? person this DO work TO DO give food -OB children self

dan wei : from doing / because did

-- "о„ҙ оҶҫо…ү о…ҸоҲ’оҲҹо…ҳ" (' bei li buna dan wei yawin delo ')
.. I like swimming ?? I AM good FROM DO go water

gin gin : and with

-- "о…ЎоҮӣ оҲ’о…Ҙоғё оҶўоҶўоҮҮо…Ў оҲ’о…әо…І" (' dis min wei dugi, gin gin luga dis wei ema-egon ')
.. She spoke, and, with her hand, drummed.

"li wei" : passive form

Verb that happened to subject

-- "о„ҙ оҶҫоҲ’оҮһ" (' bei li wei mugo ')
.. I am eaten

lon wei : In the middle of

-- "о„ҙ оҮғоҲ’о…Ҙ" (' bei lon wei dugi ')
.. I'm in the middle of talking

Conjuctions

Conjunctions define a relationship between the clause before it, and the clause after. If the subject is the same, it doesn't need to be indicated on both sides.

  • la : while / because / junction / context
  • esi : if

-- bei wei yawin la, vin o gein uwein
.. since I'm going, you can stay

-- bei wei yawin esi, vin o gein uwein
.. If I go, you can stay.

-- bei li beni yawin la, vin wei gein uwein
.. after I go, you can stay

The first clause can be something as simple as a "dis"

-- dis la, bei wei yawin
.. when this happens this I'm going

Vocatives / Imperatives

The word "o" can be used as both a polite call before a name, to ask for their attention.

-- o [ye] zan buna .. Oh Good person

or as the beginning of a request

-- o [wei] lugin ei dis .. Oh look at this.

The 'ye' and the 'wei' can be dropped, but may be said.

To request an action not happen. use va before the verb marker.

-- o va-wei yawin .. Oh don't go

Or in more urgent cases, va can come before 'o'. But many people want to be polite before negative.

-- va-o yawin .. don't go

Yes or No Questions

Yes or no questions, are asked by using the "particle not particle" structure

-- vin li va-li buna dan bei?
.. do you like me?
?? you ARE not-ARE good FROM me

To answer "yes", is "li", and "no" is "va-li"

-- vin wei va-wei yawin?
.. Are you going?
?? you DO not DO go

"yes in this case is "wei", and "no" is "va-wei"

information questions

To ask an information question, simply replace the information you with to know with "vemi".

If possible put "vemi" in the first item of the sentence, though this is not a strict requirement.

-- lon vemi?
.. Where

-- vemi wei yawin?
.. Who is going?

-- dan vemi ye vin wei zon.
.. how do you know?

-- wei vemi ye vin gin elin liawa bei
.. What are you doing with my hat?
?? DO what SUB you WITH hat me

-- gin yasin vemi wei buli ei bengo
.. How does one make a car
?? WITH way what DO make OB vehicle?

Numbers

Numbers before another object are cardinal, and dicatate the amount of an object.

5 cars : оҶҜо„ҫВ (' lan bengo ')

Numbers after an object are ordinal and mark place or position.

The 5th car : о„ҫоҶҜВ (' bengo lan ')

Number words are :

0: оҮҝВ (' vas ')
1: оҶЎВ (' gi ')
2: о…ҹВ (' di ')
3: оҮҫВ (' van ')
4: оҮңВ (' mo ')
5: оҶҜВ (' lan ')
6: оҲӘВ (' zeis ')
7: оҲҺВ (' vos ')
8: оҮҒВ (' lis ')
9: оҶ·В (' lein ')

10: оҮӮВ (' lo ')
1000: оҲҰВ (' za ')

оҮҝВ (' vas ') to оҶ·В (' lein ') act as decimal digits, and numbers may be written and spoken in this way.

345 : оҮҫоҮңоҶҜ

However, when writing larger numbers, оҮӮВ (' lo ') and оҲҰВ (' za ') can be used to mulitply the number before them, and set the place for the next digit.

13 : оҮӮоҮҫ or оҶЎоҮҫ
103: оҮӮоҮӮоҮҝоҮҫ
130 : оҮӮоҮӮоҮҫ
133 : оҮӮоҮӮоҮҫоҮҫ
303 : оҮҫоҮӮоҮӮоҮҝоҮҫ

оҮӮ before оҲҰ allows sub 1000 separations at large numbers

30,000,000 : оҮҫоҮӮоҲҰоҲҰ

оҶўВ (' gin ') can be used to separate parts of a number if the number has separate parts

3003 : оҮҫоҲҰ оҶўоҮҫ or оҮҫоҲҰоҮҝоҮҝоҮҫ

оҲҘВ (' yowa '), that is "power" can be used to give very large numbers

5 *10 ^ 28 : оҶҜоҮӮо……оҲҰоҲҘоҶ· (that is: 50 of 1000 ^ 9)

Auxiliary Particles

Auxiliary particles have a special job to do but their role is unlike any other character and have special rules for each of them.

They are:

  • "о……" (' bi ') : of
  • "оҲ•" (' wi ') : purpose
  • "о…ң" (' deso ') : except / but / however
  • "оҲӣ" (' yani ') : or
  • "о…°" (' egin ') : also / same

"о……" (' bi ') - of

bi has two main jobs

  • Seperate items.
  • Dependent clauses

Normally a noun or adjective is defined as

A B C -> meaning (A of B) of C

but if we add a 'bi' we get

A bi B C -> A of (B of C)

compare :

  • "оҲЁоҲ©о…ӣ" (' zan zega deni ') - (man of money) of small - the small wealthy man
  • "оҲЁо……оҲ©о…ӣ" (' zan bi zega deni ') - man of (money of small) - the poor man.

For dependent clauses.

'bi' can be followed by any other particle, and mean "that" referring to an intrinisic property

  • "оҲ“о…ҳо……оҲ’оҮ’" (' weno-delo bi wei meli ') : ducks that kill / killer ducks

"оҲ•" (' wi ') - purpose

"оҲ•" is used to help separate a "cup of water", and a "water cup".

  • "о…Үо…ҳ" (' bugi delo ') : Container of Water
  • "о…ҮоҲ•о…ҳ" (' bugi wi delo ') : Container whose purpose is water

The latter may not have any water in it.

" о…¬" (' edo ') - Which

" о…¬" is used for properties not intrinsic to an object.

"оҲҢоҲ– о…¬оҲ’оҮ’ о…іоҶ–оҲІо„ҙоғё оҲ’оҮҮ о…іоҶ©о…– оҮғоҮҮо…Ўоғ·" (' vo-wuwi edo wei meli ei gela-zo bei, wei luga ei guli-deli lon luga dis. ') The 'cat', that killed my fish, has blood on it's hands.

"о…ң" (' deso ') - Except/But

"о…ң" can be used like a particle to mean "except"

"о„ҙ оҲ’оҮһоҮЎо…ңоҶ–оҲІ" (' bei wei mugo muli deso gela-zo ')
-- I eat everything except fish

"о…ңоҶ®" (' deso la ') allows you to begin a sentence with "however".

"о„ҙ оҲ’оҲҹ о…ҺоҮӢ о„Ҙо„ҳо„‘о„Ўо„ҳоғёо…ңоҶ®оғёоҮҪоҲЁ оҮғоҮӢо…Ў" (' bei wei yawin da man ziavi, deso la, va-zan lon man dis ')
-- I went to the park, however, there was no-one there.

It can be used with negation to mean "nothing but","only" or "just". The va can apply to the particle or to deso directly

-- bei va-wei deso mugo ei gela-zo -- bei wei va-deso mugo ei gela-zo
.. I only eat fish

-- bei wei mugo va-ei deso gela-zo dis -- bei wei mugo ei va-deso gela-zo dis
.. I eat only this fish

-- bei wei bena ei dis va-da deso vin -- bei wei bena ei dis da va-deso vin .. I give this to only you

"оҲӣ" (' yani ') - or / choice

"оҲӣ" can be used to separate options

"оҲҠ оҲ’оҮһ о…іо…ҳ о„Ҙо„ҳо„‘о„ӣо„‘оғёоҲӣо…ҳо……оҲ°оҲҗоҮһ" (' vin wei mugo ei delo ziana, yani delo bi zi wa-mugo ')
-- you do consume tea OR coffee? -- would you like to drink tea OR coffee?

It can be used as a verb to mean "decide".

"о„ҙ оҲ’оҲӣоҮһ о…іо…ҳ о„Ҙо„ҳо„‘о„ӣо„‘" (' bei wei yani mugo ei delo ziana ')
-- I choose to drink tea

"оҲӣоҶ®" (' yani la ') can mean "otherwise".

" оҮӨоҲғо…ү о…іо…ҳ о„Ҙо„ҳо„‘о„ӣо„‘оғёоҲӣоҶ®оғёо„ҙоҮҪ оҲ’оҶ•оҮһ" (' o vei buna ei delo ziana, yani la, bei va-wei gein mugo ')
-- Oh cook well the tea, otherwise I can't drink [it].

"о…°" (' egin ') - also / same

"о…°" can mean "also"

"о„ҙо…° оҶҫоҲғ" (' bei egin li vei ')
-- I too am hot

It can also mean "the same"

"о„ҙ оҶҫо…°оҲҠ" (' bei li egin vin ')
-- I'm the same as you

"о…°оҶ®" (' egin la ') can mean "moreover / again"

"о„ҙ оҶҫо„»оҶұоғёо…°оҶ®оғёо„ҙ оҶҫо„»оҮһ" (' bei li belin lebi, egin la, bei li belin mugo ') -- I'm tired, moreover, I'm hungry.

Prefix Particles

Prefix Particles do not break the flow of meaning, but are always followed by another word that completes them. Because of this "bi" is not needed to separate these words from any words before them. In the Romanisation, it is appropriate to hyphenate the join.

these are :

  • о…©В (' edin ') : atom
  • о…әВ (' ema ') : Sound
  • оҶҸВ (' ga ') : colour
  • оҶӣВ (' geni ') : shape
  • оҶ©В (' guli ') : body part
  • оҮҸВ (' megi ') : distance
  • оҮҪВ (' va ') : negator
  • оҲҢВ (' vo ') : animal

When joined to each other the child prefix has its own clarifier

  • ga-(vo-ezan) : Wolf-coloured
  • ga-(vo-zo) : Animal coloured

оҮҪВ (' va ')

"va" before a particle, says the whole coming particle is false.

-- bei va-wei yawin .. I'm not going / I don't go

-- dis va-li buna
.. this is not good

-- bei va-wei zon .. I don't know

"va" before a verb is equivelant to "un" in english

-- bei wei va-zon
.. I unknow / I forget

-- va-o va-zon .. don't forget

  • va-zo : none / nothing
  • va-deso : only
  • va-zon : forget
